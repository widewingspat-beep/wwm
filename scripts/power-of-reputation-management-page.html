Why is a good reputation important for businesses?


Studies show that:


  • Half of consumers question a business’s competence if they have a bad experience and write a review about it.
  • Almost all buyers look at online reviews before making a purchase.
  • 69% of people looking for jobs will not accept a job offer from a company that has a bad reputation.
  • Most executives think that improving search results can help grow revenue.

But these stats only show a small part of what happens in the real world. But there are some general truths that apply to all businesses. A company’s main factors are its products or services, brand, and reputation. These factors affect how the company does business, including selling products and finding the right employees.

What is reputation management?


A reputation is how people see a business or a person. It’s how society, customers, employees, or the general public views them. People’s opinions of a company are subjective and evaluated qualitatively as either good or bad, not numerically.

A reputation is based on people’s opinions, which are influenced by many factors, including actions, statements, associations, social media, third-party commentary, and especially Google search results.

Your company’s or brand’s reputation is its most important asset. But often, a brand’s online reputation is not the same as its reputation in the real world.

This is because search results are organized by a computer program that looks at many different factors to decide how to arrange the results. So, if you don’t take action, your search results won’t get better.

Managing your reputation is important to protect and improve your brand’s online presence. It’s like a digital fortress that protects your business and makes sure you have positive reviews on all the review platforms.

Managing your reputation can help you improve almost every part of your business. However, it is a highly technical process that requires expertise and collaboration across multiple disciplines.
