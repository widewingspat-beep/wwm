One important detail that people often overlook is the idea of anchor texts. This text will explain what anchor texts are, their types, their role in SEO, best practices for using them, and how they influence link-building strategies.

What are Anchor Texts?


Anchor text is the clickable word or phrase in a hyperlink. It takes you to another webpage or a different section of the same webpage. They are usually different from the other texts because they are underlined and highlighted differently, typically in blue. Anchor texts tell users and search engines what the linked page will be about, so they know what to expect when they click the link.

Types of Anchor Texts

  • Exact-match anchor text: It consists of the exact keyword or key phrase that the linked page points to. For example, link to a page about content writing tips using “Content Writing Tips” as the link.
  • The anchor text is a partial match. It includes a slight variation of the main keyword or key phrase of the linked page. For example, if you click on “Tips for content writing,” you will be taken to a page with “Content Writing Tips.”
  • Branded Anchor Text: The product is sold using a well-known brand name. For example, if you click on a link to YouTube, you will be taken to the YouTube homepage.
  • Generic Anchor Text: Phrases that don’t describe anything, like “click here” or “read more,” are not descriptive.
  • Naked URLs: The URL is used as the anchor tag, like “https://www.example.com.”
  • Image Anchors: When a picture links to another page, the picture’s alternative text becomes the link.

The importance of clickable text in a hyperlink for SEO.

  • Context and relevance: They tell both users and search engines exactly what the content of the linked page is. This helps search engines understand how well a page matches what people are searching for, which can change its ranking.
  • Connect different profiles. If a profile has many links of different types, search engines will see that the site is getting links naturally and is a reliable source of information.
  • Making the website easier for people to use: Well-built anchor texts can improve the user experience by making it easier to navigate a website and find the necessary information. This can reduce the number of people who leave the site quickly and encourage more people to stay on the site longer.
  • Make your website show up higher in search results. Google’s PageRank algorithm looks at how many links there are to a page and how good those links are. This helps show how important a page is. Anchor texts with keywords can help a page appear higher up in search engine results.

Best Practices for Using Anchor Texts

  • Diversity Anchor Texts: Don’t use exact-match anchor tags too often. Search engines might see this as a trick, and it could cause problems. Try to have a mix of all types.
  • Be Descriptive and Relevant: When adding text, make sure it describes the page you’re linking to. This makes it easier for people to use your website and helps search engines organize and rank your page.
  • Avoid Generic Anchors: While they can be helpful sometimes, generic anchors like “click here” don’t add much value for SEO. If you can, use anchor texts that describe what you’re linking to.
  • Use branded anchor texts when they make sense. Branded anchors can help people recognize your brand and are safer to use because they won’t hurt your ranking on search engines.
  • Make image anchors better: When you use images as links, add a description of what they show. This will help improve the page’s SEO.

Anchor Texts and Link Building


When building links to your website, it’s important to have high-quality, relevant links, as well as meaningful text in the link. They tell search engines that a page is relevant to specific queries, which affects the page’s ranking.

Balancing anchor text distribution in your link profile is a tedious task. Using too many exact-match anchor tags could hurt your site’s ranking in search engines. The goal is to create a profile that looks natural and reflects how real users share your content.

Impact of Anchors in SEO

  • Anchor text is important for search engines to understand what your website is about. This helps them show your website higher up in the search results.
  • It increases the chances of getting organic backlinks because high-quality, relevant content is more likely to be linked with descriptive anchors.
  • It makes it easier for people to use the site, which is good for search engines.


However, if anchor texts are not used correctly or are overused, it can lead to negative results for SEO.

Anchor texts are a basic part of SEO, but many people don’t understand how important they are. They help users find the right website and give search engines information about the linked page.

You can improve your website’s search engine rankings, user experience, and online visibility by using anchor tags the right way, having a variety of anchor text profiles, and focusing on creating high-quality and relevant content.

As with all SEO practices, the key to success is finding the right balance, relevance, and focusing on providing value to your users.
