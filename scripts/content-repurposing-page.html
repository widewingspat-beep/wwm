The Art of Repurposing Content


Content repurposing is when you edit or rearrange existing content (like a text, image, video, or podcast episode) to fit into different types of media or for new uses. It's an effective strategy that can help you market your business more efficiently without having to start from scratch. The key to repurposing lies in finding ways to reach different parts of your audience.

Why repurpose content?

  • Make it easy to find: People understand content in different ways. These differences depend on things like culture, education, money, age, and country. If you change content into different formats, you can reach more people.
  • Improve your website's search engine ranking (SEO): Having content on different platforms will help your website show up more often in search results and attract more links to your website.
  • Reinforce the message: If you say the same message many times, it will start to feel like it's stuck in your head. It's also a good idea to change the way you say something now and then. This helps make your message more memorable and keeps your audience from getting bored.
  • Save time and resources: Instead of starting from scratch, which would take a lot of time and resources, it makes sense to modernize existing material to make it more efficient.

Clever content repurposing strategies

Convert blog posts into infographics.


Online users prefer images and videos over text. So, it's important to capture the key points and turn them into infographics. This helps engage your customers and get your message across. Pinterest and Instagram are popular social media platforms that can be used for this purpose.

Make videos using written content.


Music videos with creative titles can also be effective. People prefer watching videos to reading the same information. You can reach thousands of people on social media like YouTube, TikTok, Facebook, and Instagram by turning blog posts, customer testimonials, case studies, and success stories into music videos. Videos can be short, moving drawings or long, detailed videos to teach people something.

Create podcast episodes by recording interviews and conversations.


People use audio media like podcasts when they're doing other boring tasks at the same time. If you turn your interviews, discussions, success stories, panel debates, or expert panels into podcast episodes, you can attract new audiences to engage with your content while they're on the go.

Convert your blog posts into e-books.


E-books are becoming more popular in the digital world. People prefer soft copies to hard copies. You can collect all your blog posts and put them together in an e-book. Then, you can offer the e-book for free to get people's contact information (leads) or sell it as a digital product.

Get the main ideas from longer social media content.


Longer pieces of content like blog posts, reports, and articles are a great source of material for social media snippets. You can share interesting facts, confusing quotes, or statistics. You can do this on your own social media accounts, like Twitter, LinkedIn, and Facebook. We can also use Instagram. When we post, we include a link to the full article.

Change testimonials into case studies.


Testimonials are not just for show; they prove your team's hard work and success. We can develop these testimonials into detailed case studies to gain trust and show the whole journey of commitment, enthusiasm, and victory.

Use webinar content to create training modules.


Have you ever hosted webinars? If so, you can turn the recorded sessions into training modules. These modules can be used to help the next generation learn about your expertise in more detail.

Turn internal reports into useful information about the industry.


Information from within a company can provide valuable knowledge about the industry. If you put this information into papers and blog posts, you can show what your company knows.

How to repurpose your content in the best way.

  • Knowing who will see your message: You need to adjust your content so that it appeals to your audience. This means doing your research and choosing the right platforms for your content.
  • Update and refresh: Make sure the information you provide is always correct and up-to-date. If you're repurposing content, make sure it's just as good as the original to maintain quality.
  • Optimized for each platform: We adjust the content to fit the layout and optimization needs of the platforms you choose.

Content repurposing is a great way to get your marketing materials seen by more people and to keep them effective, all in a short period.
