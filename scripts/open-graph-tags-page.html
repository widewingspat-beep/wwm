Open Graph Tags: Want to use social media more often?

Learn how Open Graph Tags can help you get more clicks, shares, and engagement for your content.

In the always-changing world of digital marketing, having a strong social media presence is essential for businesses and people. Social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, and LinkedIn are still the most popular. It’s very important to share content that looks good and gets people to interact with it.

Learn about Open Graph (OG) tags. These tags can have a big effect on how your social media content appears. This guide will explain what Open Graph tags are, why they’re important, and how you can use them to improve your social media presence.

What are Open Graph Tags?


Facebook developed Open Graph Meta Tags in 2010. These tags allow any web page to become part of a social graph. Open Graph tags are pieces of code that share information about your website with social networks.

When a person shares a link on social media, the OG tags determine how the title, description, image, and URL appear. These tags go in the head section of your webpage’s HTML. You can change them to show your content in the most active way possible.

Open Graph meta tags are important.


The Open Graph meta tags control how your shared content is presented. If your website doesn’t have OG tags, social networks might pull text and images from your website. This can result in previews that are boring and not relevant.

This can lead to fewer people clicking on links, less engagement, and ultimately, poor performance on social media. Using OG tags will help you ensure that your content appears the same everywhere and looks good on all platforms.

  • Making things more visible: When you customize OG tags, they make your content more clickable, shareable, and visible on social media.
  • Get people to be more involved: Using interesting titles, descriptions, and images can get your audience involved more quickly.
  • Get more people to click on your links. If your website has appealing previews, more people will click on them. This will make more people visit your website.
  • Supporting SEO efforts: While this is not considered an exact cause, the increased activity can have a positive effect on your SEO rankings.

A guide to using Open Graph meta tags.


Integrating OG tags requires a basic understanding of HTML and access to your website’s backend. The most important tags you should consider adding to your web pages are:

  • Open graph title: This tag is used to define the main headline of your content. It appears when your content is shared on social media. It should be short, exact, interesting, and show what the page is about.
  • Open graph type: This tag tells the system what kind of content you are sharing, like an article, video, website, or image. It helps social media platforms understand the type of content being shared.
  • Open Graph image: This tag is very important because it determines what image is displayed in the preview. Choose an image that looks good and is related to your content. Make sure that your image is the right size for the different social media platforms.
  • Open graph URL: This tag helps people find the main URL of your content. It makes sure that all shares point to the same URL, so all shares add to the same metrics.
  • Open Graph description: This tag gives a short description of your content. As with the title, it should be: catchy, relevant, and attention-grabbing. It should also briefly explain the page’s content.
  • Open graph site_name: You don’t have to use this tag, but it’s a good idea. It makes your content and brand more connected by including your site’s name when it’s shared.

Tips to get the best results from Open Graph meta tags:


To get the most out of OG tags on your social media, follow these tips:

  • Use images that are high quality. Choose images that are clear and high resolution. Images like these will get people’s attention. Each social media platform has its own recommended dimensions. Make sure to create versions of your images that are specific to each platform.
  • Describe the problem in detail, but keep it short. Titles and descriptions should be short and give a good description. They should make the viewers curious without giving away the whole story.
  • Check your tags. Use tools like Facebook’s Sharing Debugger to see how your content will look when you share it. This allows you to solve any problems and adjust your tags to your preference.
  • Keep the content consistent. Make sure the tags you use to organize your page content are correct. Tags that are confusing or misleading can make it hard for users to find the information they’re looking for. This can also make people question whether your brand is trustworthy.
  • Update it regularly. As your content or brand changes, you should update your OG tags. This will ensure that your social shares always look fresh, trendy, and relevant.

Here are some advanced tips to improve Open Graph tags.


There are several advanced strategies you can use to improve your social media presence through OG tags.

  • Change how tags are set up so that they are different for different platforms. While OG tags are well-known, making them better for specific platforms can improve their performance. Twitter has its own set of meta tags (Twitter cards). If there are no Twitter-specific tags, Twitter will use OG tags instead.
  • Use rich media: For certain types of content, use additional tags like og: video to embed videos directly.

If you want more people to engage with your brand or business on social media, you can use something called Open Graph Meta Tags. There are two types of tags: basic and advanced.
