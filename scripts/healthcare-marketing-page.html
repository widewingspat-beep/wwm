How healthcare marketers are using AI


The first thing to know about AI is that it’s not intelligent. It’s not a doctor, a marketing expert, or a smart strategist. It’s more like a student who is eager to please and will quickly research and tell you whatever you want to hear, based on your instructions. They may not check the facts. If you’ve been following the news lately, you know that Google’s new AI Search has been criticized for suggesting things like eating rocks, glue, and undercooked chicken.

For this reason, all AI tools must be used correctly by people who know how to use them. If you give an AI bad instructions or tell it to pull data from bad sources, it’s no surprise the results are bad. But when used correctly, especially for data analysis, AI tools can help marketers work better, predict how well something will perform, and improve their data analysis.

Using AI for creating content and ideas


Most marketers know how AI is used. This is called generative AI. There are several options for people who want to use artificial intelligence (AI) for copywriting.

Although AI-generated copy still needs a lot of editing to be good enough, some users still find value in using AI to create copy.

Even healthcare marketers who don’t want to use AI to create their content can benefit from using it to analyze data and come up with ideas. Marketers can use AI to analyze data and see which health problems people are talking about most. AI can even help you choose topics for posts and emails that patients will find interesting.

Tools for user research that use AI


The best way to reach patients is to understand what they want. AI can automatically organize survey results and make important trends easy to see.

It can also provide a detailed analysis of all available data on how patients feel about different services, as well as identify patient patterns in how they use the website. These insights allow for a redesign that focuses on improving the patient experience. They also make it easier to divide things up to improve and personalize marketing efforts.

Once patient profiles have been identified, some AI tools can create virtual patients that mimic real patient behaviors. This provides more data points on site usage. The “average user” is an idea that may never actually exist in reality. However, it is possible to create many different profiles of users.

These tools can tell you more about how users behave, improve your current surveys, and possibly allow for new market research and feedback before a site update is released without needing human users.

Video analysis and podcast transcription by AI.


AI is great at automating tasks that would take a long time to do manually. For example, creating transcripts of podcasts and videos is a great way to do this. AI video tools can do two things.

First, they can write down everything that is in a video. Second, they can look at each part of the video and point out the most important ideas. This will help you prepare to create videos and written descriptions to share on social media.

If you’ve ever had to re-watch an interview to find the right clips to share online, you know how useful it is to have a transcript with highlights.

AI can predict the future and analyze data.


Predictive analysis is used a lot in modern healthcare marketing because most companies want to know which campaigns will work well before they launch them. AI forecasting is a useful tool that helps marketers predict future trends based on past data. This helps marketers create better ways to use their resources.

By studying data to see what patients are searching for, AI tools can identify patient patterns and calculate how likely they are to seek out certain healthcare services. This helps healthcare marketers understand which types of campaigns will perform well with certain patient groups. It also lets them reach out to specific groups with a campaign that is exactly right for them and offers the services they need.
