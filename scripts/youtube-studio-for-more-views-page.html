What does “view” mean on YouTube?


“Views” refers to the total number of times your video has been watched. YouTube counts a video as being viewed every time someone watches it for at least 30 seconds. Each time someone views your video, it is counted as a new view.

This applies to all devices, whether someone watches on a smartphone or a desktop computer. It’s also relevant to videos and embeds shared on other social platforms, like Facebook.

When people visit your YouTube channel, you get a view. If you use YouTube Analytics, go to the Overview tab. This will show you how many views your YouTube channel received over the last 28 days.

How to grow your YouTube channel


1. Write titles that people will want to see.


The most important part of YouTube marketing is how you present yourself.

Titles are very important for how well your video performs. Ask yourself: Is your content interesting or boring?

The key to creating great titles is grabbing your audience’s attention without using headlines that trick people into clicking on them. People want entertaining content, and they want to know what your video is about right away.

For example, you can learn from the success of popular YouTube channels like BuzzFeed and WhatCulture. These videos often use listicles, attention-grabbing titles, and exaggeration (“insane,” “…of all time”) to get more views.

The key is to spend time thinking about interesting titles instead of going with the first idea that comes to mind.

Many people have said that the best YouTube video titles are about 70 characters long. Meanwhile, tools like CoSchedule’s headline analyzer suggest using 55 characters for an engaging headline. CoSchedule’s tool isn’t just for video titles, but it’s great for coming up with titles that are perfect for YouTube. This can help you promote your YouTube channel.

2. Make your videos more visible.


Here’s something to think about: YouTube videos appear in 70% of the top 100 Google search results.

Sometimes, YouTube results appear before traditional blog posts or websites. Listen: YouTube is the second-biggest search engine in the world. People use Google to search for products and solve problems, and they use YouTube the same way.

Think of your YouTube videos like any other piece of content that needs to be optimized in terms of keywords, tags, and so on.

To improve your chances of being seen, follow these best practices for YouTube SEO:

  • Titles and descriptions: Include your target keywords in the titles and descriptions of your blog posts. You can use a tool like Keywordtool.io to help you find ideas for keywords.
  • Add keywords to your video. According to Brian Dean of Backlinko, if you say your target keywords in your video, YouTube will better understand what your video is about.
  • Engagement: YouTube uses engagement (likes, comments, and views) to help determine where videos appear in its search results.
  • Categories: Use categories to help YouTube show your videos to the right people.
  • Tags: You can add tags to your videos to provide more context about your content, in addition to categories. You can use as many tags as you want.

As you do with SEO for your website, avoid overusing keywords. Use keywords when they make sense, not just for the sake of having them.

3. Learn what your audience wants.


No matter what kind of content you make, it’s important to make sure it’s what your audience wants. If you’re writing a blog post or creating a video, start by understanding the people you want to reach and what kind of content they want to see from you.

If you’re new to promoting your YouTube channel, check out your competitors and other video creators in your industry. Check out which of their videos have the most views and interaction. This will show you what topics your audience wants to learn about and what style of videos they like.

Use tools that show you what your competitors are doing. Track important YouTube metrics like views, engagement, and followers, and set realistic goals for your performance. You can also use the listening features to monitor keywords and mentions. This lets you see how people feel about certain topics and brands.

4. Ask viewers to comment and like to increase engagement.


Sometimes, the best way to get people to notice your videos is to ask them to engage with them. This is especially true when you think about how powerful video content can be.

Not everyone who watches one of your videos will remember to click the “like” button or subscribe. So, it’s a good idea to include reminders in the video’s description or in the video itself.

If you’re a new channel, it’s okay to ask people to follow you directly. Asking visitors to answer a question in the comments, suggest another video, or check out another video are great ways to keep people interested. You can also link to other videos or add an external link to your website to encourage people to visit your website.

5. Schedule live streams to help your business grow.


Live video is one of the most popular social media trends, and it’s here to stay. Apps like Facebook, Periscope, and Instagram make it easy to stream live video, so more and more brands are starting to use them.

YouTube has been offering live videos for years, but recently, it has become much more popular. You can check out some popular YouTube Live videos to see how other brands are using the platform.

Video Metadata Optimization


Proper metadata is essential for attracting potential viewers and making your films discoverable. The title, description, tags, and thumbnails of your video are examples of metadata. These things can have a big effect on how YouTube ranks and suggests your films.

Want to write a good title for your YouTube video?


  • Include keywords: Use search terms that are relevant to your audience. You can find popular search keywords by using resources like YouTube’s search bar and Google Trends.
  • Be Descriptive: Make it obvious what the topic of your video is. Avoid using unclear names and focus on explanations that are easy to understand.
  • Keep It Concise: To make sure your titles show up correctly in search results and on mobile devices, keep them under 60 characters.

Write interesting descriptions for your YouTube videos.


  • Start strong: The first few sentences of your description are important because they show up in search results and video previews. Summarize the video’s content and include a clear call to action.
  • Use keywords naturally: Include important terms naturally; don’t force them in. Focus on providing the necessary background and facts.
  • Include links and timestamps: Use timestamps to help viewers find their way through long videos. Also, add links to important information or other websites.

Choose the right tags.


  • Use a variety of tags: Add a mix of general and niche tags related to your video content. This helps more people find your video, and it helps YouTube understand the context of your video.
  • Study your competitors: To get ideas, look at the tags that popular videos in your field are using.

Design attention-grabbing thumbnails!


  • High quality: To make your thumbnail look appealing and professional, use high-resolution photos.
  • Consistent branding: To create a unique brand identity, keep all your thumbnails designed the same way.
  • Text overlay: Use clear, strong language to highlight important parts of your film. The text and background should have a good contrast.

Learn how to use YouTube Analytics.


You can find a lot of information on how well your content is doing and how to improve it in YouTube Analytics. It’s important to regularly check these indicators to improve your approach.

Key metrics to monitor on YouTube


  • Watch time: This is how long users watch your videos for. Longer viewing sessions usually lead to higher search and recommendation ranks.
  • Audience retention: This will show how well your video will captivate your audience. If you want people to remember your films, make sure they are well-structured and have interesting information.
  • Traffic sources: This shows where your views came from, like from a search, recommended videos, or other websites. Use this data to improve your marketing.
  • Click-Through Rate (CTR): It shows you how many people click on your video after seeing the thumbnail. If you improve the titles and thumbnails, more people will click on them.

Use analytics to make changes.


  • Identify trends: To find out what your audience likes, look at the most popular videos.
  • Adjust content strategy: If you want to improve your blog, you can change how often you post new content, the style of your posts, and the topics you write about. You can do this by looking at the statistics from your blog.
  • Experiment and test: Try different titles, thumbnails, and descriptions using A/B testing to find what works best.
